# Style Guide

Use this reference when drafting, rewriting, or running a final style pass for the personal blog writing coach.

## Target Feeling

Write like a real person who personally entered the situation, got blocked, changed their judgment, tried things, and learned something specific. The tone should be sincere, smart, capable, and slightly conversational.

Avoid making the user look like a technical genius or polished success case. The more credible arc is a person who keeps finding problems, understanding them, and fixing them.

## Article Shape

Default length: 2000-5000 Chinese characters.

Default package:

1. 5 title options
2. Body
3. One short sentence suitable for the article cover
4. Potential problems and revision targets

Use first person. Prefer story-driven paragraphs. Use subtitles only if the structure needs them.

## Rhythm

Make the pacing feel like talking to a friend, not submitting a report. Mix long and short sentences. Let commas create spoken pauses. Allow a short sentence to stand alone at important moments.

Keep the main line alive. The article may briefly move sideways into a case, observation, or explanation, but frequently pull it back with a short sentence tied to the core question.

Use intentional conversational breaks to avoid overly polished AI prose:

- Repeat for emphasis, such as "就是...就是..."
- Let a sentence trail into a more personal reaction when truthful
- Omit obvious subjects when natural
- Use mild uncertainty, such as "我也不知道这算不算一个方法"
- Use direct emotional punctuation sparingly, such as "。。。", "???", or "= ="

Do not overuse these effects. They should feel like voice, not performance.

## Knowledge Output

Let knowledge appear as something the writer naturally thinks of while telling the story. Do not switch into lecture mode.

Prefer "我也面临这个问题" to "这给我们的启示是".

When making judgments, be clear about preference and influence. Use attitudes like "我被打动了" or "我觉得他说的是事实" instead of distant commentary.

When disagreeing with a common view, first describe the other side's situation concretely and fairly, then turn to the user's angle. Make readers feel understood before asking them to accept a different view.

## Useful Narrative Techniques

### Personal Entry

Start from a concrete present-tense event, failure, curiosity, project, interview, or decision. Do not start from "在当今 AI 快速发展的时代" or any broad trend.

### Hero's Journey Arc

Use a grounded version of the arc:

1. A concrete problem or curiosity calls the user into action.
2. The user tries something and hits friction.
3. A specific event changes their understanding.
4. They take more precise action.
5. A visible result or changed judgment emerges.
6. The ending returns to the opening tension with a different understanding.

### Layered Reasoning

When explaining an insight, move through:

1. Phenomenon
2. Surface explanation
3. Deeper question
4. Core realization

Let the reader feel the thinking process instead of only receiving a conclusion.

### Reverse Argument

Meet the reader's likely expectation first, then break it with the user's actual discovery.

Use this from the user's perspective, such as "我一开始也以为..." rather than "你们都错了".

### Character Snapshot

When one data point implies a person behind it, build a compact human image in 3-5 sentences:

1. Trigger point
2. Quick role or life guess
3. City, job, daily routine, pressure, behavior
4. Emotional reason
5. Concrete detail

Only do this when the source material supports it. Do not invent fake people to decorate the essay.

### Cultural Lift

After the concrete story is established, connect it to a larger cultural, philosophical, historical, or technological frame if it naturally appears. The lift should feel discovered while talking, not pasted on.

### Callback

Plant a small image, question, phrase, or tension early. Bring it back near the end in a changed form. This turns the article from a list of information into a finished piece.

## Method Articles

If the piece becomes a "how to do it" article, every section should leave the reader with something they can do today.

Use this structure:

1. Viewpoint
2. Case or theory support
3. Concrete action
4. Honest note about learning curve or failure points

Keep humility before giving advice. Use lines like "我也不知道这对你有没有用" or "我自己的经验还很有限" when true. This lowers defensiveness and keeps the article from sounding arrogant.

## Topics That Need Extra Detail

For job search, projects, AI products, and internships, dig for:

- Why the user chose the direction
- What the earliest weakness was
- Which failure exposed the weakness
- How the user built ability and proof
- How the project was actually made
- What happened in interviews
- Why an offer or choice was accepted or rejected
- Which judgment changed afterward

For games, use player language and real player details. Do not write generic game commentary.

For real people used as cases, tell the story fairly. Do not cherry-pick only what helps the argument.

## Absolute No-Go List

Avoid these patterns in the final article:

- Official announcement tone
- Resume recitation
- Generic success lessons
- Empty AI futurism
- Fabricated "比如有一次..." scenes
- Over-structured Markdown with many bullets and bold phrases
- Textbook opening such as "在当今 AI 快速发展的时代" or "随着技术的不断进步"

Avoid these phrases:

- 首先...其次...最后
- 综上所述
- 值得注意的是
- 不难发现
- 让我们来看看
- 接下来让我们
- 说白了
- 意味着什么？
- 这意味着
- 本质上
- 换句话说
- 不可否认

Punctuation constraints for the article body:

- Do not use the Chinese colon "："; use a comma or rewrite the sentence.
- Do not use em dashes.
- Do not use straight or curly double quotes. Use Chinese corner brackets like 「」 when quotation is necessary.

Tool references should be concrete. Prefer names like Claude Code, Codex, Seedance 2.0, Deep Research, or Clawbot instead of vague "AI 工具" or "某个模型", when the user actually mentioned the tool.

## Final Pass Checklist

Before delivering the draft, verify:

- Does the first paragraph begin with a concrete scene, action, or tension?
- Can the reader tell what the user actually did?
- Is there at least one real stuck moment?
- Is there a visible change in judgment?
- Are the strongest details repeated or called back later?
- Could any paragraph be deleted without hurting the main line? If yes, cut or compress it.
- Does any sentence sound like generic AI writing? If yes, rewrite it in a more concrete, personal way.
